About Elon Musk

  • Born on June 28, 1971
  • Raised in a wealthy but abusive family in Pretoria, South Africa
  • Ruthlessly bullied at school
  • Created a video game called “Blastar” at the age of 12
  • Attended Stanford University for Ph.D. in applied physics but dropped out two days later to pursue entrepreneurial ventures during the Internet boom
  • Company owner of Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink Zip2, X.com (formerly Twitter), and other more ventures
  • Became the officer of the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) in 2024

The Boring Company

Founded in 2017, The Boring Company is inspired by Musk's frustration with traffic congestion in Los Angeles. He envisions a network of underground tunnels that could facilitate high-speed transportation so as to reduce surface traffic. He hopes to develop new tunneling technologies that make tunnel construction cheaper and more efficient, hence making underground transport more viable for different cities.

Tesla

Founded in 2003, Tesla focuses on producing electric vehicles and clean renewable energy products, with an emphasis on sustainability and innovation. Known for its high-performance electric cars, Tesla aims to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy. Musk believes that the use of automated machines and artificial intelligence will help make driving more efficient, affordable and safe in the near future.

SpaceX

Founded in 2002, SpaceX concentrates on the development of advanced rockets and spacecraft. Musk aims to reduce space transportation costs and enable the colonization of Mars, achieving significant milestones such as the first commercial spacecraft to reach the International Space Station (ISS) constructed by The European countries, USA, Japan, Canada and Russia.

Neuralink

Founded in 2017, Neuralink is a neurotechnology company established with the vision of developing advanced brain-machine interfaces (BMIs) that could enable direct communication between the human brain and computers. Musk aims to address neurological conditions, enhance cognitive abilities, and eventually facilitate a symbiosis between humans and artificial intelligence.
